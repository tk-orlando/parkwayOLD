Information We Collect From Your Use of the Site

Parkway collects information about the visitors to the Site. For example, when you visit the Site, Parkway may collect your domain name and IP address, your browser version, the URL from which you arrived at the Site, and the URL to which you go upon leaving the Site, as well as the pages you visit while viewing the Site. IP addresses are numbers that are automatically assigned to your computer, and in many cases may be used to identify you.

Parkway may also use "cookies" as part of its interaction with your browser. Cookies consist of files of information that we place on your computer and later access to track your  activity. Cookies can make your use of the Site more pleasing and efficient by, for example, "personalizing" your user interface or identifying which parts of the Site you have previously visited. Many browsers enable you to refuse cookies or to determine if and how cookies will be accepted by your computer. If you stop or restrict placement of cookies, you may still use the Parkway Site, but some features may not operate correctly or at all. Parkway may also use third party providers to provide cookie-related services to Parkway and both Parkway and such third party providers may also use other tracking technologies to monitor your use of the Site that will function even if you disable cookies.  

The Site is not designed to recognize or honor  "do not track" instructions that may be turned on in some browsers.

Parkway uses the information collected by the methods described above to help diagnose problems with its server, to administer and improve the Site and to respond to improper or unauthorized use of the Site. The information is also used to help identify you and to gather broad demographic information, some or all of which Parkway might share with affiliates and third parties. Parkway also uses the information to keep track of the information you have seen on the Site. We may further personalize your experience by using your Site behavior to shape any recommendations we may make about content (including advertising) that might be of interest to you. We may also monitor traffic patterns and Site usage to help us develop the design and layout of the Site.

Your California Privacy Rights

A business subject to California Civil Code section 1798.83 is required to disclose to its California customers, upon request, the identity of any third parties to whom the business has disclosed personal information for the third parties' direct marketing purposes within the previous calendar year, along with the type of personal information disclosed.

If you are a California resident and would like to make such a request, please submit your request in writing to the Parkway Privacy Office at 390 North Orange Avenue, Suite 2400 Orlando, FL 32801 or email@pky.com.  Please note that under California law, businesses are only required to respond to a customer request once during any calendar year.
